✅ Preferred: transport upright at all times

Whenever possible, the Standup 2.0 should be transported in an upright, vertical position. Horizontal transport is a last-resort option only, not standard practice.

Always default to upright transport when possible

Use a dolly to move the unit to the installation location

The Standup 2.0 has casters on the bottom, but these are designed for short-distance positioning only. Use them for the final 5 feet of movement into place. For any longer distance, use a dolly.

Use a dolly. Casters are for the final 5 feet only.

If horizontal (last-mile) transport is necessary, all of the following must be met:

🛣️

Last-mile delivery only. Horizontal transport is permitted only for the final leg. The unit must not be transported horizontally over long distances.

🔄

Back only, never sides or front. Laying the unit on its sides or front can cause cracks, dents, and internal component damage.

📦

Nothing on top. No objects should be placed on top of the machine during horizontal transport.

⏳ Wait 4 hours before powering on

After any horizontal transport, the unit must stand upright for a minimum of 4 hours. The coolant in the chiller needs time to settle. Powering on early can cause permanent damage.
